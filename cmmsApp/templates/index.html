1. Food Packaging Plants

Hygienic Packaging Lines for Food Products

Food packaging plants handle snacks, beverages, frozen foods and similar products. These environments typically prioritise hygiene, contamination control and compliance with food safety requirements.

  • Designed for clean handling and washdown-friendly layouts where required
  • Supports consistent output quality for consumer-facing products
  • Packaging formats may include sachets, pouches, trays, bottles and cartons
  • Controls and materials selected to align with food-industry expectations

2. Pharmaceutical Packaging Plants

Controlled Packaging for Medicines and Healthcare Products

Pharmaceutical packaging requires precision and controlled conditions. Plant design commonly focuses on packaging integrity, traceability and meeting regulatory requirements.

  • Controlled environments and structured processes
  • High focus on accuracy, verification and packaging integrity
  • Common formats include blister packs, bottles, vials and cartons
  • Documentation and validation support are typically essential

3. Consumer Goods Packaging Plants

Packaging That Balances Shelf Appeal and Product Protection

Consumer goods packaging plants support household products, cosmetics and personal care items. Packaging is often designed to look attractive while still protecting the product through storage and transport.

  • Supports premium presentation and consistent branding
  • Options for labels, wraps, cartons and tamper-evident formats
  • Packaging designed to reduce damage and leakage risk
  • Flexible lines for multiple SKUs and packaging sizes

4. Industrial Packaging Plants

Heavy-Duty Packaging for Industrial Materials and Equipment

Industrial packaging plants handle large or heavy products such as machinery, chemicals and construction materials. Solutions may involve crates, drums, metal containers and bulk packaging systems.

  • Robust handling for high weights and harsh environments
  • Bulk packaging options for powders, pellets and chemicals
  • Supports safer material transfer and reduced product loss
  • Often integrated with forklifts, conveyors and dispatch systems

5. Flexible Packaging Plants

Film, Bag and Pouch Packaging for Modern Product Lines

Flexible packaging plants specialise in films, bags and pouches. These are widely used across food, beverage and personal care, offering lightweight packaging with strong convenience and branding benefits.

  • Formats include pouches, sachets, pillow packs and sealed bags
  • Supports high-speed output with consistent sealing quality
  • Material options can be selected to match product and shelf-life needs
  • Common in consumer-facing industries due to lower packaging weight

6. Primary and Secondary Packaging Plants

Primary vs Secondary Packaging Explained

Packaging plants are often designed around two packaging stages. Primary packaging directly encases the product, while secondary packaging groups products for handling, storage and distribution.

  • Primary packaging: bottles, cans, pouches and other direct contact packaging
  • Secondary packaging: cartons, cases, pallets and bulk handling systems
  • Secondary packaging improves logistics efficiency and protection during transport
  • Plants may include one stage or both, depending on production requirements
