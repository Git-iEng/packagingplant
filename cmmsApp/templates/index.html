Boiler classification

Boiler Types & Operating Characteristics

Boilers are classified by gas and water flow paths, circulation method, operating pressure, orientation and application. We align these technical characteristics to your thermal demand and process.

Flow path

Water-Tube Boilers

Water flows inside tubes while hot gases pass over them. Suitable for higher pressures and larger steam capacities—used in power plants, refineries and major process industries.

High pressure Large capacity
Flow path

Fire-Tube Boilers

Hot gases flow through tubes immersed in water. Compact and robust, they are ideal for low and medium-pressure steam and hot water in food, textile, healthcare and commercial buildings.

Compact Low/medium pressure
Energy source

Electric Boilers

Use electrical energy to generate steam or hot water. A clean option where flue emissions are restricted, or where electricity tariffs favour electric heating for smaller loads.

Zero on-site emissions Quiet operation
Pressure

Low, Medium & High-Pressure

Solutions for low-pressure heating systems, medium-pressure process steam and high-pressure or supercritical units for power generation and demanding industrial duties.

Heating & process Power generation
Circulation

Natural & Forced Circulation

Both buoyancy-driven (natural) and pump-driven (forced) circulation boilers are deployed based on height, layout, pressure requirements and response time.

Stable operation Process responsive
Application

Stationary, Portable & Marine

Stationary plant boilers, portable units for temporary or remote sites, locomotive and marine boilers, configured with the right mounting, orientation and controls.

Site-specific Flexible deployment

How we work

We follow a structured approach—from load assessment and type selection through to supply coordination and commissioning support—so your boiler installation is safe, efficient and compliant.
